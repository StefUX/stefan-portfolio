Research

I conducted an extensive research in theoretical findings about educational psychology of learning. In this field, one author Zimmerman points out with his model of self-regulated learning.

Self-learning model

Self-regulated learning is a cyclical process, wherein the student plans for a task, monitors their performance, and then reflects on the outcome. The cycle then repeats as the student uses the reflection to adjust and prepare for the next task.

The forethought phase: This is also known as a planning phase, where learners set achievable short- or long-term goals, choose strategies that best address a pursue those goals, and assess their beliefs and interest to the learning environment. The performance phase: Learners implement strategies to make ongoing adjustments in order to progress towards their goals via self-monitoring and self-control. The self-reflection phase: Learners evaluate the effectiveness of their strategies and examine their satisfaction on the learning tasks. Feedback from this self-reflection phase will then apply to the start of the next SRL cycle.

I discovered another two university researchers that dealt with applying Zimmermans self-learning model in online learning platforms, Rebecca Cheng, Ph.D. and Iris Poon.They applied the self-learning model on two different e-learning platforms: Duolinguo and Codeacademy. By analyzing both findings I managed to get insight and define the must haves in my application:

Questionnaire

To challenge the theoretical findings, I made a questionnaire covering the applications functionalities that based on the Zimmermans model are necessary for success. In demographic aspect, I targeted the primary target audience that was given in the brif. 30 high-school students answered the questionary. I summarized the following findings (hypothesis) :

  • Our user doesn’t know what UX is
  • Online learning familiar concept (because of Covid)
  • Majority users find it pointless if they can’t measure their progress
  • Users want to connect with other users / mentors
  • Our user doesn’t have the motivation nor determination to go for a long-hour courses
  • Our user isn’t confident about his/her capabilities for pursuing UX/UI career
  • 100% openess for using a learning app
  • Our user needs a daily reminder for using the app, lack of self-discipline acknowledgement

From sketches to Hi-Fi

On 3rd week I mocked up the product from sketches all the way into high-fidelity prototype in two days. Due to the time limitation, I only highlighted the major features and flows within the product, but they are sufficient for the final presentation and to gain a holistic understanding of the user experience.

As the client requested in the brif 3 onboarding processes were required: one free, and two paid modalities.

Once selected, the user has an option to start the quiz or watch a lesson. Watching a lesson isn’t obligatory, but gives additional points and for gamification purposes it stimulates loyal user to watch short video tutorials.

As mentioned before, every day has different modality for knowledge transfer. This example shows answer choosing from 4 different pictures and the outcomes. Then I introduced the popular tinder swipe (left or right) which is very familiar for our target users and good for the learnability of the overal app.

Scenario User Testing

Once completed with with a fully clickable high-fidelity prototype I went for a user testing scenario to find out eventual flaws in the design or the flow. I used the Loop11 online tool for user testing. Thinking aloud method was determined in order to stimulate the user to express loudly his opinions while interacting with the interface. I shortly explained the purpose of the research to my user tester. I gave him a scenario starting point and I stressed out that talking loudly and explaining the reasoning behind every click is crucial and from that moment I stopped talking. Here I defined the pain points of my current design which need to be adressed in the future:

  • Onboarding: I will definitely try the free version first, but what knowledge do I get for free
  • Registration - why I just can’t connect with my google account (only one click)
  • I prefer the swipe answers
  • Home page- why I can’t decide how much time to spend on the app (like in Duolingo) - Jacobs law
  • Networking- What is the thing with the QR code

Limitations: Due to the very short deadline for project delivery I wasn’t able to do the iterations.

Scenario User Testing

Once finished with the testing, the user-tester was given a 10 question System Usability Scale (SUS) to answer. The result was 82 points with acceptability range from 80. The reason I decided to go with the SUS was to determine whether I am really following the user-centered design principles and go in line with the user needs.

Summary

Sustainable e-learning systems are dependent on competent self-regulated learners. On the other hand, applying Zimmerman’s self-regulated learning model in an e-learning system can teach self-regulation and guide students’ work by instilling the process of planning, practicing, evaluating, and adjusting. This in turn allows students to assume full control and accountability for their learning. For the purpose of this project, I implemented the well accepted functionalities and gamification methods that Duolinguo uses, in order to reduce the application learnability time, reduce the users cognitive load and create an intuitive and user-friendly design. Once on the website, the user won’t be bothered with learning how to use the app, his only focus will be the actual learning which is our primary objective.

Learnings

Have a realistic time schedule

Due to the time limit, I did not have a chance to iterate. Nevertheless, this was my first do alone UX project in a very short deadline. Despite for the tight schedule, I went through the user research, ideation, and prototyping phases, which helped me rapidly gain a whole picture of how a product is created in the real-world industry. I must admit my work is far from perfect, but I think I managed to go by the book and create a solution that has relevant research done in logical steps.

Team members matter

I have been through all the pressure, the frustration, and the constraints and the many hours spent on this project. I found out, UX/UI is a team process, especialy for larger and complex projects.

Choosing the right research method is a challenge

I know I must always have the answer to Why? I chose this method. Although I managed to do my research in a logical steps, I still have the dilema whether different method would be more appropriate. I guess confusion fans away with experience.
