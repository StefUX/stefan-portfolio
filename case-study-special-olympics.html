Special Olympics

A website dedicated to athletes with special needs and their care-givers, allowing them to find and participate in special sports events, while also providing exposure for attracting partners, donors, and volunteers.

Jump to results

The challenge

Although the Special Olympics of North Macedonia is a highly active organization, they didn’t have any digital presence, which made it harder for them to share their message, find donors and partners, and most importantly, create a platform dedicated to their athletes.

The solution

Leaning into principles of inclusive design, I created an experience with the athlete’s needs in mind. My main goal was to share the message first, then allow the community to easily contribute to the main goal

My role

Entire product design from research to conception, visualization and testing

The target users

Primary

Athletes with special needs and their caregivers

Volunteers and community members

Representatives of related NGOs and possible partners

Research

Competitor analysis

As the first step of my research process, I decided to conduct a competitor analysis. Our stakeholders at the Special Olympics of North Macedonia are connected with other international organizations, and they have provided me with a list of websites of similar organizations:

www.specialolympics.org
www.somi.org
www.poraka.org
www.special-olympics.be
www.specialolympics.at

User interviews

With already predefined target users, I went for an interview with a relevant representative of each category. I conducted 3 interviews.

Goals

What kind of information every target group is looking for on the website?
What kind of information do each target group consider a must ?
Define the specific threats of each target group for the purpose of persona creation

Findings

Athletes with special needs and their caregivers
  • Due to the specific nature of the subject caregivers need information about sporting opportunities for their child
  • They need contact with other parents for advice and support
  • They need contact info with organizations representatives
Volunteers and community members
  • They need information about volunteering opportunities
  • They want to get insight into the organization's activities and whether its work is suitable for them
Representatives of related NGOs and possible partners
  • They acquire information about Special Olympics activities, agenda, and scope of work
  • They need info about possibilities for cooperation and mutual projects

User personas

With the findings from the interviews and competitor analyses, I created user personas. I focused on their needs in context with the organisation's work, but also with their possible pain points.

User journey map

A user journey map helped me to better understand the respective needs of each persona. The originally planned syllabus was greatly adapted as there were unnecessary and unfavorable steps coming to light.

Information Architecture

With the previous research, I was able to create a clean architecture taking into account every functionality and simple, intuitive navigation.
